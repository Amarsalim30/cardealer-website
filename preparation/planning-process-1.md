To design a budget‑conscious car‑dealership site that converts, it helps to blend proven marketing tactics with the features your client liked on **smartdreamcars.co.ke**.  That site uses an interactive search bar and highlight cards (e.g., “Imported units in Mombasa,” “Traded‑in Cars” and “Available for Importation”) to guide visitors toward inventory, displays customer testimonials, and explains its financing and trade‑in options clearly.  Industry research underscores that vehicle detail pages must offer strong, action‑first calls to action such as **Get Today’s Price**, **Book a Test Drive**, **Calculate Payment** and **Value Your Trade**; that lead forms should ask only for essential details (name, email, phone); and that visible financing applications and trade‑in options increase inquiries.  A case‑study of 1 billion dealership impressions found that action‑oriented wording (“Get Today’s Price,” “Book / Schedule a Test Drive,” “Calculate Payments,” “Value Your Trade”) consistently outperforms passive phrases.

### Recommended structure and features

**1. Home / Landing page**

* Use a hero banner with a **search filter** (make, model, price) to let shoppers immediately search inventory—similar to Smart Dream Cars.
* Feature three or four category cards (e.g., **Imported Units**, **Traded‑in Cars**, **Available for Importation**) with brief descriptions and “View all” buttons to channel visitors toward inventory.
* Include a **“Why choose us”** section to highlight differentiators (e.g., wide range of brands, trusted by clients, fast & easy financing).
* Add a **gallery of sold units** or customer testimonials with photos to build credibility.
* Briefly explain **purchase/financing options** (cash purchase, hire purchase, bank/SACCO financing, trade‑in) and include a call‑to‑action button leading to a full financing page.

**2. Inventory and categories**

* Consolidate the navigation to **Search Inventory** with filters for new cars, used cars, imported units and traded‑in vehicles. Real‑time inventory sync is essential for trust and SEO.
* Each category page should display vehicle cards with key details (make, model, year, price, mileage) and a photo, linking to the vehicle detail page.

**3. Vehicle Detail Page (VDP)**

* Provide high‑quality photos, specifications and vehicle history.
* Offer short, single‑purpose forms for each action:

  * **Get Today’s Price** (price request/quote)
  * **Book a Test Drive**
  * **Calculate Payment / Apply for Financing**
  * **Value Your Trade**
  * **WhatsApp Chat / Call Now**
* Keep each form simple—request only name, email and phone—to maximize submissions.
* Include a phone number with a click‑to‑call link and a WhatsApp icon for immediate contact.

**4. About / Trust and Contact pages**

* Use an About page to tell the company’s story and display customer reviews, years in business and partner logos.
* The Contact page should clearly show phone numbers, email addresses, showroom locations and business hours.

**5. SEO and analytics**

* Ensure each vehicle has its own optimized URL with descriptive titles and location keywords.
* Install Google Analytics 4 and Google Search Console to track visits, lead submissions and search visibility.
